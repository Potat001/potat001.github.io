As you can see by my Youtube channel, I have made plenty of videos. Starting in late 2018, my first video was some raw gameplay footage without editing or commentary, since I didn't know how to edit videos at that time. But I then installed an editing software called Wondershare Filmora (with the free version and the giant watermark, no less) and began cutting up my videos and adding text and sound effects to them. Then I experimented and got better and better at editing and applying tricks to make it more engaging to watch, eventually switching to Adobe Premiere Pro somewhere in 2021-2022 and getting better with that. Premiere Pro was much more complex than Filmora however, so I had to set myself into it and figure things out (which, there is still a lot I need to google), which I eventually did. This was the time when I posted This video, which wasn't my first highlight video, but it was the first one I spent a significant amount of effort on (timing the music with my actions for example), and, the video got really popular, eventually reaching 76k views as of currently. I then continued making highlight videos, in a short or long format, and eventually a sequel, which at first didn't gain too much traction, but it skyrocketed after some time, now being my 2nd most watched video clocking in at 115k views as of currently. So, I was the Valheim youtuber now, where if I posted other types of games/videos, with some exceptions, I would get significantly less views. But I had ~300 hours in Valheim, so I knew I wouldn't run out of clips to post anytime soon. So, I kept making Valheim videos, with the occational other video, and I was getting a ton of views. But I started to get burned out on the game around 2022 and the quality of my videos suffered as a result. So, I decided to stop making Valheim videos and focus on making videos I genuinely enjoy making instead, and also not forcing myself to pump out videos at a consistent rate, further reducing the quality of my videos. And, the quality and passion for making videos has come back and I'm much more happy with my videos now, even if I took a year break from making videos. So, if you're going to get into video editing, make sure that you enjoy what you are doing and not forcing yourself to do it. You might get less views from it, but it's 100% worth it.
